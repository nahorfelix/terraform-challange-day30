# Day 30 Reflection - What Changed in 30 Days

## What changed in how I think

Infrastructure stopped feeling like one-off manual setup and became an engineering system I can model, review, and reproduce. I now think in terms of repeatability, state safety, drift control, and reliable workflows instead of quick console fixes. Plans, module boundaries, and state discipline now guide my decisions before I touch production resources.

## What I am most proud of

The hardest part I pushed through was troubleshooting multi-step Terraform failures (permissions, backend/state issues, and module wiring) without giving up. Solving those issues built confidence that I can handle real-world infrastructure problems under pressure. The multi-region deployment days were difficult, but finishing them made everything after that feel more structured and manageable.

## What comes next

After certification, my next step is applying this workflow on a production-style project: reusable modules, remote state, CI validation, and environment separation. I want to contribute these practices to a real team setup and keep improving with hands-on delivery. My immediate goal is to carry this same process into team projects so infrastructure changes are safer, reviewable, and repeatable.
